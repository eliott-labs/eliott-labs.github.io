Monaco 2022 F1 GP

After attending the 2022 Monaco F1 Race, I've written an Article which addresses what I learned about the track, marshals and the city. The experience overall was fantastic and the memories will stay with me for a while.

    After I was lucky to attend the 2022 Monaco Grand Prix, I reflect on what I learned about the city, the track and a questionable trend amongst the marshals. Getting around Monaco is surprisingly easy when the track is open. The streets are wide open and it’s extremely easy to get from one side of Monaco to the other. On the flip side though, when the track is closed, it is remarkably more difficult to get anywhere. Forget trying to get anywhere by car as the traffic is flooded by a mix of people, taxis, and police presence. Travelling from and to Monaco is relatively easy by taking the train, although we found it most busy at the start of the day and obviously late nights. Going to Monaco, the train is only busy in the morning and then calms down in the afternoon. If you wish to leave Monaco, you’ll find yourself in a very long line waiting to get on the train. Pretty much any hour after 6pm the train system is packed, and you’ll be waiting a while to get anywhere.
Enough about transport now, I found the city and the shops/stores around fantastic to visit. With many shops having vintage motorsport items and clearly many memories made there. Lots and lots of Formula 1 related merchandise can be bought at any time and anywhere around the city.

    The track looks remarkably different when it’s being used by the general population and your regular traffic. Something you can immediately notice is just how warm the track itself is, the black asphalt absorbing the summer heat. Other things that really surprised me was just how much the elevation changes From Turn 1 up and then down into the Fairmont Hairpin (Loews). TV coverage really doesn’t give the uphill justice and the descent is pretty steep as well.

    Something you won’t find lacking in Monaco are luxury vehicles, supercars and classics. Lamborghinis, Mercedes 300SL, you name it, you’ll see it. Monaco overall and during the GP Weekend is a haven for car lovers and you’ll have no shortage of pictures with these cars. If you have a keen eye, you might even spot Connor McGregor Lamborghini Yacht that I was lucky enough to snap a picture with. Parked right outside the Team Hospitalities, it was extremely difficult to miss in it’s yellow/gold colors with black highlights.

    Onto a more serious topic and maybe slightly worrying. The night before Race Day walking past La Rascasse (bar/club) we saw a worrying amount of Marshalls still in their notorious Orange Automobile Club de Monaco Overalls drinking and partying with other members of the public. This raises a safety concern of having hungover Marshals with delayed reactions and impaired decision making. Upon research, I have not been able to find whether Marshals are limited to a certain number of drinks and what the protocols on Marshals partying the night before F1 and its support Races are. Nevertheless, it’s still an interesting discussion about safety officials partying and drinking before arguably one of the more dangerous tracks of the year.
